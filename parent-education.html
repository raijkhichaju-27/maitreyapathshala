Parent Education

Fostering a Collaborative Learning Community between Parents and Waldorf Education

A Shared Journey

At Maitreya Pathshala, education is a collaborative journey built on trust, respect, and shared intention.

We believe that the journey of education is not confined to the classroom. Parents are the child's first teachers, and their active participation is vital to holistic development. To strengthen this partnership, we offer a thoughtful programme of parent education. Through parent and teacher education, we nurture a culture where every adult involved in a child's life is informed, inspired, and empowered to support their unfolding potential.

Understanding Child Development

We host a variety of sessions for parents across the school year, addressing developmental milestones at every stage of childhood

Early Childhood Development

Supporting the unique needs and natural growth rhythms of young children. Learn about play-based learning, healthy routines, and creating nurturing home environments.

The Ninth-Year Threshold

A pivotal stage where children begin to experience a sense of individuality and emotional depth—also known as the "9-year change." Guidance for navigating this important transition.

Adolescence and Beyond

Providing insights and strategies to navigate the complex emotional landscape of the teenage years. Communication techniques and supporting emerging independence.

Skill-Based Parent Workshops

In keeping with our hands-on Waldorf ethos, we offer creative and practical sessions for parents

Woodwork and Artistic Activities

Enabling parents to engage with the crafts that form part of their child's learning journey. Learn basic woodworking, painting techniques, and handcrafts used in our curriculum.

Parent Study Groups

Facilitating shared reading, dialogue, and reflection on parenting, education, and childhood development. Explore Waldorf philosophy and child development theories together.

Seasonal Crafts & Festivals

Learn to create seasonal decorations and understand the significance of Waldorf festivals. Bring the rhythm of the seasons into your home environment.

Community Involvement

Building a supportive network of parents engaged in their children's education

Many of our parents actively contribute to school life by sharing their expertise through guest talks, project sessions, or field trips. This enriches classroom learning with real-world insight and helps children see learning as a community-wide endeavour.

Parents can participate in classroom activities, assist with school festivals, join our parent council, or share professional skills that complement our curriculum. This collaborative approach strengthens the bond between home and school.

Grade-Specific Parenting Sessions

Age-appropriate guidance for each stage of your child's educational journey

Kindergarten Parents

Understanding the importance of play, rhythm, and imitation in early childhood. Learn why Waldorf kindergarten is intentionally free of academic instruction, giving children time to be fully, joyfully young.

Grade 1-8 Parents

Supporting the transition to formal learning and navigating the nine-year change. Understand the class teacher journey—where one dedicated teacher stays with the class—and support your child as their horizon expands outward from self to community.

Class 9-10 Parents

Guiding through adolescence and academic challenges. Learn how we honor this profound transformation, providing intellectual rigor and SEE preparation without losing artistic wholeness and inner resilience.
