This document outlines how the comprehensive curriculum details from the "Journey" document were integrated into the Maitreya Pathshala website. 

Because we were restricted from adding new HTML pages, the curriculum information was woven directly into existing pages to enrich the site content without overwhelming the layout.

## 1. Updates to the Home Page (`index.html`)

The homepage's **"Classes Available"** section was updated to reflect the premium, Waldorf-aligned language from the provided document.

**Changes Made:**

*   **Class 9-10 Card:** Updated the title (from Grade 9-10) and description to highlight the focus on the upper school: *"Age 14-16 | A rigorous and holistic curriculum emphasizing integration, depth, and SEE preparation."*

#COMMITMENT
Commitment to the school
The education of children is a collaborative between families, teachers, and school. The home environment and home life is fundamental to the growth and development of all children and the quality and depth of the education they receive in school. The family culture strongly influences and colors the classroom experiences and vice versa. We ask that parents support their children through active involvement and a conscious commitment to the school.
We ask the parents;
•	Familiarize themselves with the practices and philosophy of Waldorf education for children.
•	Attend parent education meetings, festivals, and other school activities whenever possible
•	Bring the children to school on time
•	Time to time check your child’s project or work at home
•	Send your child to school with the full and proper dress
•	Pay tuition and fees in a timely manner
•	Volunteer your services to the school. No contribution is insignificant or too small.
•	Strive to create a calm, orderly and rhythmical home environment that supports the need of your child so that they are fully prepared and ready for school.
Supportive home rhythms include;
•	Limiting media exposure (TV, computers, Video games, etc) particularly on school nights
•	Eating wholesome and nutritious foods (particularly breakfast)
•	Proper sleep rhythms (at least 10 hours of sleep, particularly on school nights)
•	Setting and enforcing age appropriate boundaries for behaviors
•	Encouraging creative, imaginative play over intellectual activities at this stage of development 
•	Guiding them with warm and loving authority
Open channels of communication between parents and teachers are strongly encouraged and send an unspoken message to the child that there is team of loving adults supporting and caring for them. Open, direct, and frequent communication with your child’s teacher, sharing observations and feedback provide helpful insights into the individual child. Morning communications at drop off should be as brief as possible for all of us the preparing for the day ahead before the lesson begins at 9.30am. We try to protect and show respect for the children by distinguishing between conservations intended for adults and conservations appropriate for children. Examples of inappropriate conservations for children would include, talking “about” the children or discussing teachers in front of them, particularly in time of conflict. Please make arrangements during school hours when teachers are available for more in depth conservations or those of personal or sensitive nature.
Regular parent education meeting are an important part of the teacher/parent relationship and an essential aspect of our efforts to build and strengthen community. Parent and class meeting are excellent opportunities to enhance parent/teacher communication as well as building relationship among the parents. In these meeting we discuss the daily life of the children at school, deepen our understanding of Waldorf education and child development, announce and plan upcoming school events, review school expectations, and provide important information.
Meetings are always informed well in advance of upcoming meetings.
 Seasonal festivals are celebrated in the school and are outlined in the school calendar. Your child’s teacher will provide more detailed information about any upcoming events. Parents interested in celebrating or observing special festivals with their child’s class are encouraged to contact the teacher directly
Parents Guidelines
Parents and students are expected to thoroughly read all written correspondence from teachers and administration in a timely fashion. Parents are expected and required to maintain up to date contact information including email addresses and mobile phone numbers by contacting the administrative directly. 
Parents are responsible for providing the medical history detail of their child and are encouraged to update them as necessary. Medical information should be handed directly to the teacher and are held confidentially. Extra curriculum activities are a regular part of the school experience for all students and any restrictions must be provided by the parents to inform the teachers.
At the conclusion of each term, parents are requested to meet the teacher in one to one basis with prior appointment to updates of the progress of their children. Waldorf schools do not rely on grading children’s work but class teacher will write a daily assessment of each child with comprehensive picture of the development, as well as social skills and a description of the material covered in each subjects. If a student is struggling in any subject, the parents can expect to be contacted by the teacher directly as soon as the challenge is identified for develop strategies of support.
Reports may be issued at the end of the school year. As a reminder, these reports are written for the parents and should not be read by younger children.
In the high school letter grades are given on a scale of A to E
A minimum of 90% attendance in the school working days is compulsory for all students. An explanation of absences for one or more days must be submitted in writing by the parents.
The school is responsible for covering the telephones from 8.30am to 4.00pm every day school. The school are not responsible for conveying or delivering non – emergency messages. Teachers and faculty individually provide contact information and hours of their availability for conversation at the beginning of the school year.
All gazette holidays observed by the government are followed by the school year. (Holiday list)
In any event of any emergency situation, school may be delayed or closed. The decision for school closure or delay will be made no later than 7.00am and the school will answer any calls.
Tuition payments
Parents have options for tuition payment. Tuition may be made in advance in one payment for the entire year or parents may opt to pay in two equal installments or quarterly installment or monthly installment. Payments are due on the 7th of each month or term beginning in April.
The board of school is responsible for the financial health of the school and requires that all families meet their obligations in a timely manner. Tuition fees account for 90% of the school’s income and thus late payment may impair the ability to pay teachers and staff, run programs, and can adversely affect the normal operation of the school.
Fees of any nature, once paid are non – refundable and non – transferable. Any family which encounters difficulty in meeting their financial obligations must immediately inform the administration in writing. The school will make every effort to find a mutually agreeable arrangement on an individual basis and all financial information is strictly confidential.
Conflict Resolution
When differences lead to conflict, we have in place a series of steps to address concerns and find meaningful resolution.
1.	Direct communication with the teacher
2.	Direct contact with the administration
3.	In particularly grievous cases, a third party mediated meeting can be arranged. Essential to any meaningful resolution of conflict is the conscious exercise of flexibility by both parties and timeliness, for conflicts left unaddressed only get bigger and can quickly grow out of proportion.
First Grade Readiness
The readiness of children for enrolment in the first grade is an important consideration for it not only impacts the social health of the class but also can be a determining factor for the child’s success in meeting the challenges of learning in classroom environment.
 Withdrawal of students
We at Maitreya Pathshala value every student and appreciate the many families make to provide a Waldrof education for their children. There are times however, when it may be necessary to withdraw an enrolled student. Normally we do not expect the children to be withdrawn during the ongoing academic session, April to March. However should it be necessary, parents are expected to give 3 months notice before the child is withdrawn from school.
